Educational Journey: From Zero to Understanding Code

My path into the D4 Informatics Engineering program began from a unique starting point. Coming from a science background in high school, I had no programming foundation at all. Initially, the world of coding felt very foreign and challenging. However, a strong sense of curiosity pushed me not to give up. I took the initiative to learn continuously, both by actively asking lecturers and friends, and by studying independently outside of class hours. Slowly but surely, I began to grasp key concepts like UI/UX, Frontend, and even Backend. This journey taught me that one's background is not an obstacle when accompanied by a strong will to grow.

Organizational Experience & Leadership

Beyond my academic focus, I am also actively involved in the Informatics Engineering Student Association (HIMA), where I have been entrusted with the role of Head of the Media and Information (Medinfo) Department. In this position, I act as the lead designer and driving force behind all of HIMA's digital communication strategies. I hold full responsibility for the planning and execution of content across various platforms, ranging from social media (Instagram Feeds & Stories, YouTube), video production (event short films, podcasts), to the management of the official HIMA website. I also manage publications for every event, both for internal audiences and for building relationships with media partners. This role requires me to blend a creative vision with effective project management, honing my skills not only in leadership but also in strategic communication in the digital era.

Achievements and Interests: Uniting Art and Logic

Outside the world of coding, I have a great passion for visual arts and storytelling. I have continuously honed my skills in design, videography, photography, and short film scriptwriting throughout my time at university.This passion led my team and me to win a short film competition at the university level. Professional Experience: Event Documentation Volunteer at IFBC Surabaya

Seeking to expand my experience beyond the academic sphere, I also actively contribute as a volunteer. One of my most valuable opportunities was joining the Documentation Division for the Info Franchise and Business Concept (IFBC) expo in Surabaya. This was a large-scale exhibition featuring dozens of franchise companies, serving as a major hub for business professionals.In this role, I was responsible for capturing the key moments throughout the event. A significant highlight was the opportunity to document the official opening ceremony, which was attended by the Vice Governor of East Java, Mr. Emil Elestianto Dardak. This experience not only honed my technical photography and videography skills in a dynamic and professional environment but also trained me to perform effectively under the pressure of a major event.
